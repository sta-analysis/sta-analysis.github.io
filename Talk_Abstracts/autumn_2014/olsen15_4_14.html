The purpose of this talk is to introduce a class of subsets of \(\Bbb N\) defined in terms of the asymptotic behaviour of the digits in the \(N\)-ary expansion of the integers, and to study the "fractal" dimensions of these sets. As an example we consider discrete Besicovitch-Eggleston subsets of the positive integers \(\Bbb N\) based on the frequencies of digits in the \(N\)-ary expansion of positive integers, and show that the "fractional" dimensions of these sets satisfy a formula analogous to the classical Besicovitch-Eggleston dimension formula for the Hausdorff dimension of the usual Besicovitch-Eggleston subsets of \(\Bbb R\).
