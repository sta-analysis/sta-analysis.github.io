Group Members

Core Faculty

  • Stephen Cantrell

  • Kenneth Falconer

    My research centres around the geometry of fractals and multifractals, geometric measure theory, dimension theory, dynamical systems and probability. Specific topics include self-affine sets and measures, sections and projections of fractals, random fractals and stochastic processes, and applications of fractals to other areas.

  • Jonathan Fraser (Head of Group)

    My research interests centre on fractal geometry and its connections with other areas of mathematics, including: geometric measure theory, dynamical systems, Fourier analysis, hyperbolic geometry, and combinatorics. I often work with dynamically defined fractals, including self-affine sets and Kleinian limit sets, and am interested in several geometric problems related to projections.

  • Natalia Jurga

    My research interests lie in the interplay between dynamical systems, ergodic theory and fractal geometry. More specific topics include: dimension theory of dynamical systems, random matrix products, self-affine sets, transfer operator methods and thermodynamic formalism.

  • Lars Olsen

    My research covers all aspects of fractal and multifractal geometry and geometric measure theory, including work on self-affine sets and measures, fractals defined by digits of numbers, typical structure of fractal sets and measures, etc.

  • Mike Todd

    I work in ergodic theory and dynamical systems, applying ideas from probability and thermodynamic formalism to study expansion and recurrence properties of, mostly, smooth low dimensional systems. Topics include Return Time Statistics, Extreme Value Theory, statistical stability, equilibrium states, phase transitions and transience.

Further Staff

  • Roope Anttila

    My research is primarily focused on geometry of fractal sets and measures. Some specific topics include limit sets and invariant measures for non-conformal iterated function systems, random matrix products, projections and slices of fractals, random covering sets, and quasisymmetric geometry of fractals.

  • Collin Bleak

    My research is in studying infinite groups of homeomorphisms via properties of their actions. In this study I employ tools from various fields including algebra, theoretical computer science, combinatorics, symbolic dynamical systems, and analysis. Collin sits in the Algebra and Combinatorics Research Group.

  • Spyridon Dimoudis

    I am interested in operator and spectral theory and their applications in mathematical quantum mechanics, specifically, in the analysis of non-self-adjoint Schrödinger, and related, operators.

  • Dmytro Karvatskyi (Visiting)

    My research is devoted to the study of sets having a complicated local structure, such as fractals, Cantorvals, fat Cantor sets, etc. Such sets naturally appear in various branches of mathematics. In particular, they are attractors for some iterated function systems.

  • Firdavs Rakhmonov

    My research primarily focuses on additive combinatorics, combinatorial number theory, discrete geometry, discrete Fourier analysis, fractal geometry, and geometric measure theory. Currently, I am investigating problems in vector spaces over finite fields and exploring analogous results in the continuous setting.
