Analysis Research Group

There is a strong tradition for Analysis at St Andrews, notably in the mid 20th century under the leadership of Professors Turnbull and Copson. Professor Copson retired in 1969, and the present Analysis Group was established in the mid-1990s with the appointment of K. Falconer followed by the young and motivating researchers L. Olsen and B. Stratmann. The Group has successfully consolidated itself over the past 10 years and created an exciting and vibrant environment: recruitment of postgraduate students is flourishing, currently with 8 research students, and the research output of the Group is novel and exciting, with several new strands of research being developed. The School plan envisages that a further young member of staff will be appointed very soon.

The group's main interests are:

  • Geometric Measure Theory and Fractal Geometry.

  • Dynamics and Ergodic Theory.

  • Multifractal Geometry.

  • Kleinian Groups.

  • Metric Number Theory.
  • Stochastic Processes.

  • Noncommutative Fractal Geometry.

  • Thermodynamical Forlism within Noncommutative Geometry.

  • Group Theory and Fractals.

  • Differential Equations on Fractals.
