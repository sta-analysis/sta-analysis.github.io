A 2017 result of András Máthé states that, given any degree d polynomial p : Rnv → R with rational coefficients, there is a subset E ⊂ Rn of Hausdorff dimension n/d that does not contain any v distinct points x1, ... , xv such that p(x1, ..., xv) = 0. We discuss a version of this result that applies when the coefficients of p are assumed only to be algebraic over the rational numbers.
